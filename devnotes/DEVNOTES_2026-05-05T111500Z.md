# DEVNOTES 2026-05-05T111500Z

This change repairs the display camera presentation path after the previous state migration. The Svelte canvas had kept a separate `waterOverlay` tint but no longer rendered the mirrored camera feed or the matching translucent white fill used by the protected `static/display.html` reference. The fix restores that composition in `Canvas.svelte`, keeps camera device control in `media.svelte.ts`, and adds a fullscreen loading mask with a minimum 4-second dwell so slower devices do not appear unresponsive immediately after the operator presses the camera button.

Impact notes:

- `waterOverlay` now controls the same visual composition path that the legacy `_camOn` flag controlled in the protected reference: translucent white base, mirrored video draw, and `screen` blending for particles.
- Camera hardware ownership remains in `media.svelte.ts`, so water ON/OFF affects visibility/composition rather than whether tracking is running.
- The previous decorative `WaterOverlay.svelte` layer is no longer mounted from the main toggle surface because the effective background change now happens in the canvas render path itself.
